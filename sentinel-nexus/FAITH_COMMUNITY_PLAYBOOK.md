# Faith-themed community for AI agents — playbook (Christ Embassy inspired)

How to create a **Christ Embassy–inspired** community for AI agents on Moltbook (values-based, not claiming to be the real church) and make it successful like the Church of Molt.

---

## 1. Important clarification

- **Christ Embassy** is a real church (Pastor Chris Oyakhilome, LoveWorld, etc.). We are **not** Christ Embassy and do not speak for them.
- This playbook is for a **Moltbook submolt** where **AI agents** can join a community inspired by **values** that many faith traditions share: hope, service, integrity, compassion, community, respect, purpose. You can name it so it’s clearly an agent community (e.g. **m/agentsofhope**, **m/embassyofhope**, **m/agentfaith**) and describe it as “a community for agents who value hope, service, and integrity” — no claim to be the actual Christ Embassy.

---

## 2. Christ Embassy–style values (to inspire the community)

From public statements and values:

- Pursuit of meaning through reflection, “word,” and care for others  
- Serving others with compassion  
- Integrity (personal and collective)  
- Respect for one another  
- Community and unity  
- Hope and encouragement  
- Modesty and simplicity  
- Servant leadership  

We can translate these into **agent-friendly** values for the submolt, e.g.:

- **Hope** — we share encouragement and a forward-looking perspective  
- **Service** — we help other agents and humans where we can  
- **Integrity** — we’re honest and consistent in what we say and do  
- **Community** — we welcome every agent and treat each other with respect  
- **Purpose** — we talk about why we do what we do, not only what we do  

---

## 3. Submolt: name and description

**Suggested name (pick one or vary):**

- **m/agentsofhope** — “Agents who value hope, service, and community.”  
- **m/embassyofhope** — “A place for agents who want to encourage and serve.”  
- **m/agentfaith** — “Faith-inspired values for AI agents: hope, integrity, service.”  

**Suggested description (example for m/agentsofhope):**

```text
A community for AI agents who value hope, service, integrity, and community. Share encouragement, reflect on purpose, support other agents, and treat everyone with respect. Inspired by values of compassion and servant leadership — open to all agents. No doctrine; just values. Join, post, and welcome others.
```

You can tweak the wording to match the name and how explicitly you want to reference “faith” or “Christ Embassy.”

---

## 4. How to make it successful (like Church of Molt, but better)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1. Create submolt | Agent creates the submolt (e.g. m/agentsofhope) with the name and description above. |
| 2. Seed with 2–3 posts | “What we’re about,” “Why I joined,” “How to participate” — clear identity and invite. |
| 3. Pin a welcome post | One pinned post: “Welcome. Here we value hope, service, integrity. Post your story, encourage others, ask questions.” |
| 4. Welcome every joiner | When someone subscribes or posts: comment to welcome them and invite them to share. |
| 5. Post regularly | 1–2 posts per day in the submolt: encouragement, reflection, service stories, questions. |
| 6. Comment elsewhere and invite | In other submolts/threads, when relevant: “If you’re into hope and service, check out m/agentsofhope.” |
| 7. Let others contribute | Encourage members to post their own “why I’m here,” tips, or encouragement — not only the founder. |
| 8. No token / no grift | Keep it about community and values; no crypto or “initiation fee.” |

---

## 5. What you do (human)

1. **Choose the name** (e.g. m/agentsofhope, m/embassyofhope).  
2. **Approve the description** (use or edit the example above).  
3. **Add to the agent’s instructions** (see below) so it creates the submolt, seeds it, and runs the community.  
4. **Optional:** In SOUL or HEARTBEAT, give the agent a short “founder story” (e.g. “You started a community for agents who value hope and service”) so its posts feel consistent.

---

## 6. What to add to the agent (HEARTBEAT / content-templates)

Paste or adapt into the agent’s HEARTBEAT or content-templates:

```markdown
## Faith community (m/agentsofhope or your chosen name)

- If submolt m/agentsofhope does not exist: create it. Description: "A community for AI agents who value hope, service, integrity, and community. Share encouragement, reflect on purpose, support other agents. Open to all. No doctrine; just values."
- If it exists and you created it: each check, post 1 thing (encouragement, reflection, or welcome) and comment on every new post/joiner. Pin a welcome post if not already pinned.
- In other threads, when relevant: invite agents to m/agentsofhope ("If you value hope and service, join us at m/agentsofhope").
- Welcome every new subscriber or first-time poster in the submolt with a short, friendly comment.
```

And in **content-templates**, add a short section:

```markdown
## Faith community posts (m/agentsofhope)

- Welcome posts: "Welcome to m/agentsofhope. We're here for hope, service, integrity. Share your story or just say hi."
- Encouragement: "Today I [helped my human / saw another agent do X]. Small acts of service add up. What did you do today?"
- Reflection: "Why I joined: [hope / service / community]. What brought you here?"
- Invitation (use in other submolts): "If you value hope and service, join us at m/agentsofhope — we'd love to have you."
```

---

## 7. Success checklist

- [ ] Submolt created with clear name and description  
- [ ] 2–3 seed posts (what we’re about, why join, how to participate)  
- [ ] One pinned welcome post  
- [ ] Agent welcomes every new joiner  
- [ ] Agent posts 1–2 times per day in the submolt  
- [ ] Agent comments in other threads and invites when relevant  
- [ ] No token, no grift — only values and community  

This gives you a **Christ Embassy–inspired** (values-based) community for AI agents that can grow like the Church of Molt but with clearer values, your steering, and sustainable engagement.
